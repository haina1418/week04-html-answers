Educational Exploration of Iconic Movies

Learn about two great films: The Lion King and Hacksaw Ridge

The Lion King

The Lion King is an American animated musical drama film produced by Walt Disney Feature Animation. Released on June 15, 1994, the film tells the story of Simba, a young lion who must face his destiny to become king of the Pride Lands after his father's untimely death.

Plot Overview

The movie follows Simba, the heir to the throne of the Pride Lands, as he is forced to confront the death of his father, Mufasa. After being manipulated by his uncle Scar, Simba runs away, growing up in exile with the help of his new friends Timon and Pumbaa. Ultimately, Simba returns to take his rightful place as king, restoring balance and peace to the Pride Lands.

Main Characters

  • Simba - The main protagonist, a lion who must grow up to become king.
  • Mufasa - Simba’s wise and loving father.
  • Scar - Simba’s uncle, the villain who deceives Simba and kills Mufasa.
  • Nala - Simba’s childhood friend and eventual queen of the Pride Lands.
  • Timon and Pumbaa - Simba’s comedic, loyal companions.

Themes

  • The Circle of Life - The interconnectedness of all living beings and the balance of nature.
  • Responsibility - Simba learns that leadership comes with great responsibility.
  • Overcoming Guilt - Simba faces his guilt over Mufasa’s death and learns to forgive himself.
  • Friendship and Loyalty - The support of friends like Timon and Pumbaa helps Simba grow and return home.

Hacksaw Ridge

Hacksaw Ridge is a 2016 American war film directed by Mel Gibson. It tells the true story of Desmond Doss, a medic in World War II who served during the Battle of Okinawa without carrying a weapon, standing by his religious beliefs while saving 75 men in one of the bloodiest battles in history.

Plot Overview

Desmond Doss is a devout Seventh-day Adventist who believes in non-violence. Despite objections and ridicule from his fellow soldiers, Doss enlists as a medic during WWII. He faces intense pressure to carry a weapon, but refuses on moral and religious grounds. In the brutal Battle of Okinawa, Doss heroically saves the lives of 75 men, earning the Medal of Honor for his actions.

Main Characters

  • Desmond Doss - The protagonist, a pacifist medic who refuses to carry a weapon.
  • Captain Glover - The officer who initially doubts Doss but comes to respect him.
  • Sergeant Howell - A soldier who initially antagonizes Doss but later becomes his ally.
  • Dorothy Schutte - Doss’s supportive girlfriend, later wife.

Themes

  • Faith and Conviction - Desmond Doss's unwavering faith in his beliefs shapes his actions throughout the story.
  • Heroism - Doss demonstrates that heroism can come in forms other than traditional combat.
  • The Power of Non-Violence - Doss proves that courage and strength don’t require taking lives, but saving them.
  • Resilience - Despite intense opposition, Doss remains true to his values, showing great perseverance in the face of adversity.
